	Changing words doesn't necessarily change the underlying concepts -- for example, in photography,
what some companies still call a "master" flash still controls a "slave" flash.
	And sometimes people pick new words in an attempt to start fresh with neutral vocabulary,
only to find the new term picks up the baggage of the old.
"Water closet" becomes "toilet," which becomes "bathroom," which becomes "restroom."
